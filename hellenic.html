Hellenic Museum of Michigan


Refreshing the museum's web experience.



Type

Alternative Spring Break Volunteer Work

Team Size

Two UMSI students

Role

User Researcher and UX Designer

Duration

Mar 4th 2019 - Mar 8th 2019

Tools

Photoshop, Axure RP, Squarespace



THE PROBLEM

An urgent need to update the museum website


During the spring break, I participated in UMSI's five-day long Alternative Spring Break initiative, which is purported to contriute and make positive impact on the local communities with informational services.

Our client was the Hellenic Museum of Michigan, which is a volunteer-initiated non-profit organization that aims to treasure and promote the Greek culture in the Greater Detroit area and other regions in Michigan.

The museum's current website was built on a software that is no longer updated. With only one volunteer in charge of maintaining the website, the museum website is in urgent need for a new experience. Now that the museum has been under renovation, we were tasked to build a new website before the museum reopens again.

Screenshot of the museum's previous website



THE RESULT

A newly designed website experience on a easily manageable CMS


After four working days, our team conducted user research, re-scoped and communicated the solution, and delivered a completely developed new website using Squarespace, a CMS that is easy to manage.

The new website includes eight different pages.



Index


Before:

After:

Social media plug-ins display real-time updates. At the footer, there's an added menu for users' quicker navigation.

THE PROCESS

A five-day design sprint



The volunteering project was very short. In order to bring value to the client, we took the challenge to go beyond prototype and actully built a website that could be immediately deployed by the museum.

RESEARCH

Gathering data for design direction


Stakeholder Interviews


We began the project by holding several interivews with stakeholders and potential users. In the orientation meeting, we met theree different roles of the museum to understand their expectations.



User Interviews


To understand user needs, we also conducted two interviews with potential visitors of the museum. Aside from understanding their motivations and possible usae scenarios, we also performed a user test using think-aloud protocol where the interviewees completeed tasks in assigned scenarios on the previous site to see their impression on the browsing experience.

The interview protocol can be downloaded here.

After the user interview, we conducted an affinity analysis to systemize data about user behaviors and attitudes on the previous website.



Issues brought up by both users were broken links on the index page, lack of ticket information and exhibition details. Users were also not impressed by the visual presentation.

They did a really good job hiding that (exhibition details).

The style feels outdated and there is too many texts.

To remind us of design focus, we mapped the data gathered from user tesing results with the screenshots of the previous site.

Comparative Analysis


We also compared five other museum websites of different sizes, two with immigration theme like our client, to see how they present their information. Our key learnings are:

  • Showing tickets/opening hours and prices at the top of the index page
  • Detailed information should be provided for exhibition
  • Content should be image rich so visitors know what to expect



Personas and Scenarios

SCOPE AND DEFINE

Re-scoping the project and deciding on the structure


After the research, we were overwhelmed by how many items needed to be done. To completely fulfill user needs, the content of the website would have to be thoroughly researched, discussed and drafted to provide most valuable information for the users. With less than 3 working days left, we needed to decide on what tasks to be kept, and what should be set aside.

After balancing stakeholder needs and user needs, we decided to provide an immediately viable and scalable framework of the website.

Each of us first brainstormed the new organization of the website, and we were surprised to find that our organizations were very similar. By combining our versions, we presented the sitemap structure to our client in an update meeting, which was approved to proceed.

DESIGN AND BUILD

Realizing ideas to a functional website



Rapid Wireframing


We started the design by working on different pages individually. After 15 minutes of rapid sketching, we presented our wireframes of responsible pages to each other.

Build and Test


We spent the last two working days on translting ideas from the wireframs to actual website. Instead of prototyping first, we decided to directly build the final product on Squarespace due to time constraint. A style guide was developed directly on Squarespace with the following color palette.




Throughout the process, we made some design changes from our initial sitemap plan.

  • Separate 'Exhibitions' and 'Programs' tab to encourage program visibility. (The program pages were taken down in the final version because the clients would like to revise the program details.)
  • Remove the 'Contact Us' subpage from 'About', because the content is similar to 'Plan a Visit', and because the contact information should always made accessible at the footer.
  • Changed the upcoming events from calendar view to list view, because there was no new event in March, and this could confuse users.
