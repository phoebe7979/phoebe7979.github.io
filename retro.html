THE PROJECT

A comprehensive examination of the internal web application


For a usability course project, I worked in a team of four UX-focused students to provide usability consulting service for RetroQuest -- an open source web application developed by FordLabs for running weekly retrospecitves.

With no current intention to push RetroQuest to the market, the web application’s current target users are internal teams at Ford. On a weekly basis, 90-170 teams at Ford run their retrospecitve meetings on RetroQuest. With only one designer in charge of the UX of the application, the development team is seeking consulting from our team to help them thoroughly evaluate the application's usability.

THE METHODOGY

Examining the web application via six different approaches


We assessed RetroQuest comprehensively using six different methods, gathering both quantitative abd qualitative data to he lp us generate key recommendations for our clients.





INTERACTION MAP

First exploration of the application


To better undesrtand the application, we started by visualizting all possible interactions on the interface to learn how users can engage with the system in different ways. The interaction mpa also notes initial usability issues. For example, clicking through a button opens up a blank page.





COMPARATIVE ANALYSIS

Learn from direct and partial competitors



Next, we conducted a thorough analysis with 6 direct (FunRetro, GoReflect, ScatterSpoke) and partial (Trello, Basecamp, Airtable) competitors to see what they did well that can be receommended to RetroQuest.

One huge difference between RetroQuest and the competitors is that other applications have an account system. This could help users participating in multiple retro boards better access the boards.Other features that all other competitors have include comment feature and sharable link.



USER INTERVIEWS

How do users deploy RetroQuest in a retro? What are their frustrations?



In order to understand how users feel using RetroQuest, we conducted 5 semi-structured interviews with different roles at Ford. The interview protocol can be found here . We also developed personas to illustrate the major user using the application, their motivations and frustrations.




SURVEY

Gathering quantitative data to validate needs and assumptions from a larger dataset



With findings from previous research, we have gathered several theories and assumptions that could help improve the application. To see whether these considerations are really needed by the majority of the users and their attitudes about them, we distributed a survey to employees at Ford.

Within 5 days, we received 81 responses. Using Tableau and excel, we visualized the data into charts below.



HEURISTICS ANALYSIS

Taking a closer look at the application with 13 meuristics



We constructed a set of 13 heuristics from Nielsen(1995), Farooq et al. (2008), and Hermann (2009) to examine RetroQuest from general user interface design point of voiew, and it's effectiveness in computer-supported cooperative work. The heuristics are:



  • Visibility of system status
  • Match between system and the real world
  • Communication around shared work should be able to happen in real time as the work is done
  • User control and freedom
  • Consistency and standards
  • Error prevention
  • Recognition rather than recall
  • Support flexibility in the granularity of planning
  • Aesthetic and minimalist design
  • Help users recognize, diagnose, and recover from errors
  • Help and documentation
  • Support the big picture and visualization of rich material
  • Support merges and document them


In the end, we identified 18 violations, with 10 of them rated more than 3 out of 4 scores in severity level.
